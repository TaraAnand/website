Women @ CORE

Description

Women@CORE is the only dedicated program for female entrepreneurs at Columbia University. We match selected highly-motivated women who are passionate about entrepreneurship with successful female entrepreneurs in New York City. Our purpose is two-fold:
    To inspire and support undergraduate women hoping to pursue a career in entrepreneurship or explore areas such as technology innovation and venture capital by connecting them with accomplished women in these areas
    To create a community of mentors and mentees who share the goal of increasing the number of women successes in the startup environment
Programming consists of a mentorship program, panels, workshops, and a dinner series—all of which are aimed at inspiring more women to pursue entrepreneurship and providing a support network to do so. Please get in touch with either Victoria (vml2123@columbia.edu) or Jamie (js4390@columbia.edu) if you’re interested in learning more!

Events

  • The Mentorship Kickoff Mixer began the year’s activities, occuring on October 5th at Work-Bench, connecting all of the program’s mentors and mentees in one place for the first time.
  • Office Hour Dinners are monthly informal dinners that occur on Columbia’s campus, matching three to four mentors each to around 20 mentees. The dinners are designed to be a casual way for mentees and mentors to connect.
  • Women@CORE Panels and Speaking Engagements allow mentors to speak at Women@CORE or CORE panels throughout the academic year. These panels are a great way to connect with the Columbia community and share experiences in entrepreneurship.
